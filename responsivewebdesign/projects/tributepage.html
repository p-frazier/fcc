The Legend of Zelda

Acclaimed Video Game Franchise

The Legend of Zelda is an action-adventure video game franchise created by Japanese game designers Shigeru Miyamoto and Takashi Tezuka, primarily published by Nintendo. It was first released in 1986 on the Nintendo Entertainment System and has since enjoyed 16 subsequent releases spanning 34 years.

The series primarily centers around various incarnations of Link, the main protagonist. Link is typically tasked with rescuing Princess Zelda and the kingdom of Hyrule from the warlord-turned-demon, Ganon through a mixture of puzzles, action/adventure, combat, exploration, and stealth.

The Legend of Zelda is considered by some to be one of the greatest gaming franchises of all time and garners positive reviews from video game journalists and players alike. Critics laud the franchise for its varied gameplay, fully realized worlds, and thoughtful character design.
